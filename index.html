How much super should I have at my age?

Check what your super balance should be now to retire comfortably.

About this Calculator:

  • Estimates how much super you (single) or your household (couple) should have today to be on track for a comfortable retirement
  • Is based on ASFAs benchmark for retirement spending, which you can read about here.
  • Is an estimate only and should not be relied upon for making financial or product decisions

Purpose

This calculator is designed to provide a basic estimate of how much super you should currently have (single) or your household should have together (couple) to be on track for a comfortable lifestyle in retirement.

Limitations

A calculator such as this is a rough estimate; its most significant limitations that will impact the calculation include:

  • The calculator uses the ASFA “Comfortable” retirement standard as an assumption of how much combined super is needed at retirement at age 67: $630,000 for a single person or $730,000 for a couple, as at March 2026, depending on the option you select. If you are not a homeowner, or intend to retire at a different age, these figures may not be appropriate.
  • The calculator assumes constant investment returns every year. Actual returns will vary over time, which means you may need a different amount than that shown.
  • The calculator assumes that investment returns and superannuation contributions are applied annually at the end of the year; this is likely to overestimate the amount of superannuation required today.
  • Does not account for any voluntary contributions you may be making.

Salary and superannuation contributions

The calculator uses a default salary of $74,100 which is the median salary in Australia as of August 2025. For couple mode, the same default is used for partner salary. Salaries are assumed to increase with wage inflation.

Employer superannuation contributions are calculated as a percentage of each salary and are defaulted to the Superannuation Guarantee (SG) rate of 12%. Concessional contributions up to the concessional contributions cap are generally taxed at 15%. For the 2025-2026 financial year the annual general concessional cap is $30,000 per person. This calculator caps each person’s modelled concessional contributions at that amount (combined contributions for a couple can be up to twice the cap).

Administration fees and costs, and insurance premiums

Gross administration fees and costs of $62.40 per annum, plus 0.10% per annum have been included in the projections by default. These administration fee and costs assumptions are reasonable as they are aligned with the current fees and costs paid by an Australian Retirement Trust member for whom this calculator is designed to be used.

We have used a default amount of $521 p.a., increased with wage inflation each year, which we believe is reasonable as this is the default amount used in ASIC’s MoneySmart Superannuation Calculator.

Investment returns

A default investment return of 6.5% is used in the calculator, which can be varied. Investment returns are after investment fees and costs, and taxes.
